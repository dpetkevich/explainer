Intelligence per Watt: How Much Thinking Can a Laptop Buy You?

Every time you ask a chatbot a question, some machine burns real electricity to answer it — and today that machine almost always sits in a distant data center, not in the phone or laptop in your hand. This explainer asks a very physical question: how much useful thinking can a chip deliver per watt of power it draws, and has that number improved fast enough that your own device could now handle most of the work instead? To answer honestly, we have to start from the basic physics of power and energy, then work up through how a chatbot actually computes an answer, before we can see why small AI models running locally have quietly gotten good enough to take over most of what the cloud used to do.
