Intelligence per Watt: Why Your Laptop Should Answer Most AI Queries

Every time you ask a chatbot to rewrite an email, a datacenter somewhere streams hundreds of gigabytes of neural-network weights through a 700-watt accelerator. This paper measures a quiet crossover: small models on a laptop now answer 88.7% of everyday queries correctly, and their intelligence per watt grew 5.3× in two years. Follow one real query through both paths — laptop and datacenter — and you'll see why routing the easy 80% locally cuts AI's energy bill by 60–80%. Datacenter chips are strictly better, yet the whole argument comes down to one accounting identity: energy = power × time.

The basic building blocks

We take one real query and ask what it physically costs to answer. Two facts — energy is power times time, and generating text is a memory-streaming problem — fix the cost of every answer, and expose what today's cloud-only serving wastes.

Generation speed is set by memory bandwidth, not arithmetic

Why did the phone take 37 s? Watch the email reply generate token by token: each token forces the entire 10 GB of model weights through the memory pipe while the arithmetic units sit mostly idle.

Cloud serving amortizes the weight stream across users — yet still pays frontier-scale cost per query

Here is today's baseline: your email query joins 15 others on an H200 running a 235B-parameter frontier model. Batching is a real, huge advantage — drag the slider and watch joules per query collapse — but the model is still frontier-scale for a two-line email.

The new technology

With the cost model in hand, the paper builds one number — intelligence per watt — and shows the three tricks (sparse experts, aggressive quantization, model diversity) that let laptop-class models answer 88.7% of real queries.

Why it beats existing tech

Here is the puzzle: measured chip against chip, cloud accelerators beat the laptop on every efficiency metric. Yet routing queries locally saves 60–80% of energy. This part resolves the apparent contradiction with the paper's real numbers.

Chip for chip, cloud accelerators beat the laptop on every efficiency metric

A fair fight: the same GPT-OSS model, batch size 1, on laptop vs cloud silicon — and the cloud chip wins everything, up to 7.4× per joule. So how can routing queries to the loser save energy? Hold that tension.

Routing easy queries to a 20× smaller model saves 60–80% of energy — despite worse local chips

Simulate 80.2M queries a day through a router: 80.7% — including our email — go to small local models, the rest to Qwen3-235B on an H200 batched at 16. Drag router accuracy and watch the savings saturate around 80%.

Why now

None of this was possible in 2023. A memory constraint broke, efficiency compounded 5.3× in two years, and the same trend now points one tier further down — to the phone in your pocket.

Intelligence per watt grew 5.3× in two years — mostly from better models

Step through the years: Mixtral-8x7B on a 2023 workstation GPU scores 7.92×10⁻⁴ accuracy per watt; GPT-OSS-120B on an M4 Max scores 4.18×10⁻³ — 5.3× in two years. Flip to per-joule and the total jumps to 18×.
